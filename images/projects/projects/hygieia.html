Project Hygieia

ASEAN Digital Health & Climate Resilience Initiative

I also developed Project Hygieia, a digital health and climate resilience solution that placed 6th out of 144 teams in a hackathon, demonstrating my ability to apply business analysis, digital transformation, and strategic problem-solving to real-world public health challenges.

Project Hygieia proposed ADHCRI, a regional digital health intelligence ecosystem designed to help ASEAN move from fragmented public health response to predictive, coordinated, and climate-resilient regional readiness.

Project Achievement

Project Hygieia placed 6th out of 144 teams in the hackathon, finishing in the top group of competing teams. The project demonstrated a complete business analysis approach, including problem definition, business case development, readiness assessment, solution design, risk analysis, and implementation planning.

Executive Summary

ADHCRI, the ASEAN Digital Health and Climate Resilience Initiative, is a proposed federated regional health intelligence platform. It connects health data, climate indicators, disease surveillance signals, mobility patterns, country readiness, and data-confidence levels into one regional decision-support ecosystem.

Business Problem

ASEAN faces cross-border public health risks, climate-related health threats, uneven digital health maturity, and fragmented national health systems. While the risks are regional, response capabilities are often separated by national boundaries, creating delays in detection, coordination, and action.

Proposed Solution

ADHCRI does not replace national health systems. Instead, it connects them through secure interoperability, AI-supported risk detection, climate-health intelligence, readiness-based deployment, and regional governance.

The platform helps decision-makers identify rising risks, detect weak signals, assess data confidence, recommend coordinated response actions, and trigger support for lower-readiness countries.

Deployment Model

The project uses a phased, readiness-based, federated deployment model guided by ADHIRF — the ASEAN Digital Health Investment Readiness Framework. Higher-readiness countries validate the platform first, while lower-readiness countries receive infrastructure, workforce, governance, and technical support before full participation.

Key Capabilities

  • Cross-border public health intelligence
  • Predictive disease surveillance
  • Climate-health risk monitoring
  • Data confidence scoring
  • Federated data governance
  • Readiness-based implementation planning
  • Executive decision-support dashboards

Business Value

Project Hygieia showed how digital health transformation can improve outbreak readiness, reduce fragmented decision-making, support vulnerable countries, and strengthen regional climate-health resilience. The project positioned ADHCRI as a resilience investment rather than a one-time technology purchase.

Key Takeaways

This project demonstrates my ability to translate a complex regional problem into a structured business analysis solution. It combines strategy, stakeholder thinking, governance, risk management, data quality awareness, digital transformation, and implementation planning.
