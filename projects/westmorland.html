Westmorland River Watershed Project

GIS-Based Environmental and Hydrological Analysis

A geospatial project examining the terrain, hydrology, environmental characteristics, and land-use patterns of the Westmorland River watershed.

Project Overview

The Westmorland River Watershed Project involved applying Geographic Information Systems techniques to assess the physical, environmental, and hydrological characteristics of the watershed.

Spatial datasets were used to examine terrain, drainage patterns, land use, water movement, and other landscape conditions that influence the watershed.

The project demonstrates how GIS can support watershed management, ecosystem protection, environmental planning, and evidence-based decision-making.

Project Objectives

Assess the physical characteristics of the watershed
Examine terrain and drainage patterns
Analyze land-use conditions within the watershed
Interpret environmental and hydrological processes
Produce clear and informative GIS maps
Support sustainable watershed planning
