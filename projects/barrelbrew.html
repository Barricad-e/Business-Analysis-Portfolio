Barrel & Brew

Digital Transformation & ERP Process Improvement

Designed a business-led digital transformation strategy to modernize brewery operations by replacing fragmented manual processes with an integrated ERP solution. The initiative focused on improving demand forecasting, production planning, inventory visibility, quality management, reporting, and cross-functional collaboration to enable scalable growth and operational excellence.

Explore Case Study Download BRD

The Transformation Story

Barrel & Brew’s rapid growth created pressure across production, inventory, quality control, and sales fulfilment. What previously worked as a small, manual operation became unsustainable as demand increased across 500+ local restaurants.

This case study shows how I analyzed the current-state operation, identified process gaps and root causes, and designed a future-state ERP-enabled operating model to improve forecasting, production planning, inventory visibility, quality control, and management reporting.

My Role as the Business Analyst

Served as the Business Analyst responsible for evaluating existing brewery operations, identifying business and process improvement opportunities, eliciting and documenting business requirements, analyzing stakeholder needs, and designing an ERP-enabled future-state operating model. The role also involved process mapping, gap analysis, root cause analysis, solution evaluation, and the development of implementation recommendations aligned with the organization's digital transformation objectives.

Current-State Assessment

Following stakeholder discussions and process analysis, the existing brewing and bottling workflow was documented to establish a baseline for identifying operational inefficiencies and improvement opportunities. The assessment revealed several manual activities, disconnected systems, and process bottlenecks that constrained productivity and limited business scalability.
