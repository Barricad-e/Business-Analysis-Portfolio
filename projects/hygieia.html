Executive Summary

ADHCRI is a proposed federated regional health intelligence platform designed to help ASEAN strengthen public health coordination, climate resilience, and cross-border disease surveillance. The platform connects health data, climate indicators, disease surveillance signals, mobility patterns, country readiness, and data-confidence levels into one regional decision-support ecosystem.

Instead of replacing national health systems, ADHCRI works as an interoperability layer that allows countries to share approved indicators, alerts, and risk intelligence while maintaining control over their own data. The goal is to help ASEAN move from delayed, fragmented response to earlier detection, coordinated action, and predictive regional readiness.

Business Problem

ASEAN faces growing cross-border public health risks driven by disease outbreaks, climate-related health threats, population mobility, and uneven digital health maturity across member states. While these risks move across borders, many health systems still operate mainly within national boundaries.

This creates delays in detection, reporting, and coordinated response. Some countries have advanced digital health systems, while others face infrastructure, workforce, connectivity, and governance limitations. As a result, regional decision-makers may not have timely, trusted, and complete intelligence when public health risks begin to rise.

Proposed Solution

ADHCRI provides a secure, AI-supported, climate-informed regional intelligence platform that connects existing national systems rather than replacing them. It allows ASEAN countries to share approved public health indicators, outbreak signals, readiness insights, and risk intelligence through a federated governance model.

The platform uses predictive surveillance, climate-health monitoring, data confidence scoring, and executive dashboards to identify rising risks and recommend coordinated action. It helps decision-makers see where risk is increasing, where data is missing, what response is needed, and which countries may require support first.

Deployment Model

The project uses a phased, readiness-based, federated deployment model guided by ADHIRF, the ASEAN Digital Health Investment Readiness Framework. This model recognizes that ASEAN countries are not all starting from the same level of digital health maturity.

Higher-readiness countries begin first by validating the platform, testing interoperability, and establishing regional best practices. Moderate-readiness countries then expand using lessons learned from the early rollout. Lower-readiness countries receive targeted support in infrastructure, workforce training, governance, connectivity, and technical capacity before full participation.

This approach avoids a one-size-fits-all rollout and ensures that lower-readiness countries are not excluded from regional transformation.

Key Capabilities

  • Cross-border public health intelligence: Supports faster regional visibility into disease risks and public health threats.
  • Predictive disease surveillance: Uses AI-supported analysis to detect weak signals before risks escalate.
  • Climate-health risk monitoring: Connects climate indicators such as rainfall, flooding, and heat risk to potential health impacts.
  • Data confidence scoring: Flags missing, delayed, incomplete, or unreliable data so decision-makers understand the strength of the evidence.
  • Federated data governance: Allows countries to retain control of national data while sharing approved indicators and regional intelligence.
  • Readiness-based implementation planning: Uses ADHIRF to guide phased deployment based on each country’s digital maturity.
  • Executive decision-support dashboards: Gives regional leaders a clear view of risks, response priorities, and support needs.

Business Value

Project Hygieia shows how digital health transformation can improve outbreak readiness, reduce fragmented decision-making, support vulnerable countries, and strengthen regional climate-health resilience. By connecting public health, climate, mobility, and readiness data, ADHCRI helps ASEAN act earlier and coordinate more effectively before local threats become regional crises.

The project also presents ADHCRI as a resilience investment rather than a one-time technology purchase. It supports better use of resources, faster response planning, improved regional cooperation, and stronger protection for populations most exposed to climate and health risks.
