Project Hygieia

ASEAN Digital Health & Climate Resilience Initiative

A regional digital health intelligence project proposing ADHCRI, the ASEAN Digital Health and Climate Resilience Initiative, to strengthen outbreak detection, climate-health resilience, and cross-border public health coordination across ASEAN.

Project Achievement

Project Hygieia placed 6th out of 144 teams in the hackathon. The project demonstrated a complete business analysis approach, including problem definition, business case development, readiness assessment, solution design, risk analysis, and implementation planning.

Executive Summary

ADHCRI is a proposed federated regional health intelligence platform. It connects health data, climate indicators, disease surveillance signals, mobility patterns, country readiness, and data-confidence levels into one regional decision-support ecosystem.

Business Problem

ASEAN faces cross-border public health risks, climate-related health threats, uneven digital health maturity, and fragmented national health systems. The risk is regional, but the response is often fragmented.

Proposed Solution

ADHCRI does not replace national health systems. Instead, it connects them through secure interoperability, AI-supported risk detection, climate-health intelligence, readiness-based deployment, and regional governance.

Deployment Model

The project uses a phased, readiness-based, federated deployment model guided by ADHIRF, the ASEAN Digital Health Investment Readiness Framework. Higher-readiness countries validate the platform first, while lower-readiness countries receive infrastructure, workforce, governance, and technical support before full participation.

Key Capabilities

  • Cross-border public health intelligence
  • Predictive disease surveillance
  • Climate-health risk monitoring
  • Data confidence scoring
  • Federated data governance
  • Readiness-based implementation planning
  • Executive decision-support dashboards

Business Value

Project Hygieia shows how digital health transformation can improve outbreak readiness, reduce fragmented decision-making, support vulnerable countries, and strengthen regional climate-health resilience.
